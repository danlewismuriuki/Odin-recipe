cake-Recipe

Description

A cake is a sweet, baked dish made from flour, sugar, and other ingredients, and is usually baked. In their oldest forms, cakes were modifications of bread, but cakes now cover a wide range of preparations that can be simple or elaborate, and which share features with desserts such as pastries, meringues, custards, and pies.

Ingredients

  • Flour: Flour is the main ingredient in a cake. It provides the structure and body of the cake.
  • Sugar: Sugar is the sweetener in a cake. It also helps to tenderize the cake and give it a moist texture.
  • Eggs: Eggs are the binding agent in a cake. They help to hold the cake together and give it a light and airy texture.
  • Fat: Fat, such as butter or oil, helps to keep the cake moist and tender.
  • Liquid: Liquid, such as milk or water, helps to dissolve the ingredients and make the batter smooth.
  • Leavening: Leavening is what makes the cake rise. It can be baking powder, baking soda, or a combination of both.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour a 9x13 inch baking pan.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together butter and egg until light and fluffy. Beat in vanilla extract.
  4. Add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients alternately with the milk, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Beat until just combined.
  5. Pour batter into prepared pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  6. Allow cake to cool completely before frosting.
